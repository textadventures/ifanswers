I made a text adventure on The Stanley Parable last year, and ever since, I've been getting a few bad reviews from it simply because... get this... it wasn't the actual Stanley Parable game! Yes! answered Apr 17 by EatenByAGrue (75 points)

I assume people are googling 'The Stanley Parable', finding your game, and assuming it's a playable online version of another game called 'The Stanley Parable', which you appear to have copied. Don't assume everybody is looking at the URL of where they end up, or even necessarily knows what a text adventure is-- you might consider renaming it 'The Stanley Parable: Text Version' and including a link to the original game's site to make it more clear that yours is not the original. That would probably be a good idea anyway, since the way the page is now there isn't even any indication that there IS another game, beyond a copyright notice at the bottom (and I wouldn't know Galactic Cafe wasn't you unless I could figure it out from context), and so it looks like you're presenting the concept as your own work.
